Higher Time Derivatives of Position

The package SI defines subtypes for the traditional first two derivatives of the position, speed and acceleration, which everyone knows and experiences in their daily life. Of these, only acceleration can be felt by our body. For roller coasters and robotic movements, a few further derivatives are important, whose facetious names are not as standardized as the first ones:

Jerk     d3r/dt3
Snap     d4r/dt4   (Jounce)
Crackle  d5r/dt5
Pop      d6r/dt6

Of these, only especially Jerk and to a lesser extent Snap (or Jounce) can be felt by our body when we move – they are the reason of vibrations.

But there are still more derivatives. As cited in Freistetters Formelwelt, Spektrum der Wissenschaft 5.26, p.67, a book about robotic arms' positioning names the seventh to tenth derivatives, but does not give the names' origins (alternative names in parentheses from here):

Larz     d7r/dt7    (Lock)
Bong     d8r/dt8    (Drop)
Jeek     d9r/dt9    (Shot)
Sooz     d10r/dt10  (Put)

An interesting read for more details is Eager, David; Pendrill, Ann-Marie; Reistad, Nina: "Beyond velocity and acceleration: jerk, snap and higher derivatives", European Journal of Physics 37.6 (2016-10-13).
